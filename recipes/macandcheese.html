Mac and Cheese Recipe

Description

Mac and cheese is a comforting American dish featuring tender elbow pasta enveloped in a creamy cheese sauce—typically made with cheddar, milk, and butter—for a rich, velvety texture. Baked or stovetop, it often includes a golden breadcrumb or extra-cheese topping that adds a crisp contrast. Beloved for its simple, indulgent flavor, mac and cheese is a go-to at family dinners, potlucks, and weeknight meals.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz elbow macaroni
  • 2 Tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 Tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded sharp cheddar
  • 1/2 cup shredded mild cheddar or Monterey Jack

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add macaroni and cook until just al dente (about 1 minute less than package directions). Drain and set aside.
  2. In the same pot over medium heat, melt the butter. Sprinkle in the flour and whisk constantly for 1–2 minutes until it bubbles but doesn’t brown
